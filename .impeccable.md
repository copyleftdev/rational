# Design Context — Rational site

## Users
Engineers, founders, and agent builders deciding how to spend on LLM inference. Technically sophisticated, allergic to hype, persuaded by numbers and reproducibility. Reading during a coffee break or while making a real cost decision.

## Brand Personality
Precise · rigorous · quietly bold. Voice of a research desk, not a vendor. Credibility through proof (numbers, sources, reproducible math), never adjectives. Emotional goal: "I can trust this and act on it."

## Aesthetic Direction
Light mode. Editorial data-journal meets precision instrument (think high-end financial research / a scientific paper that's alive). Warm ivory paper, ink text, one confident vermilion accent, emerald for "winners". Explicitly NOT: AI dark-mode neon/glow, cyan-on-dark, gradient text, glassmorphism, templated card grids.

Fonts: Bricolage Grotesque (display), Spectral (body serif), JetBrains Mono (tabular figures only).

The signature: kinetic data — SVG charts that draw/trace/grow themselves on scroll (Intersection Observer), count-up numbers, the privacy-frontier line tracing into its wall. Motion is the differentiator, executed with restraint (transform/opacity only, exponential easing, reduced-motion honored).

## Design Principles
1. Proof over adjectives — every claim carries its number and (where possible) its source.
2. Motion serves comprehension — charts animate to reveal structure, not to decorate.
3. Light, warm, precise — ivory paper + ink + a single sharp accent; 60-30-10 weight.
4. Reproducibility is the brand — the repo + `make all` is the closing argument.
5. Never AI-slop — if it reads as "an AI made this," redesign it.
